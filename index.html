Research

  • Unsecured Lending via Delegated Underwriting: no collateral, no identity, no centralized underwriting. Sponsors stake their own credit on borrowers they trust, making fake accounts worthless by construction
  • SOLO: Liquid Staking for Solo Validators: a solo validator needs 32 ETH today. SOLO reduces that to 1.25 ETH through a permissionless LST minted against the portion of stake that can't be lost to slashing
  • zkUniswap: the first zkAMM — offloads UniswapV3 swap computation to a zk co-processor with on-chain proof verification, and captures MEV by auctioning pool locks
  • Commitment-Satisfaction Committees: solved Ethereum Foundation open problem ROP-4 — an in-protocol mechanism where attester subcommittees enforce arbitrary proposer commitments under ePBS
  • PEPC-DVT: proposer commitments enforced through distributed validator technology, with no changes to Ethereum consensus. One of the first out-of-protocol PEPC solutions
  • Making PEPC-DVT private: BLS blinded multi-signatures let the DVT network sign blocks without seeing their contents, enabling a PEPCified MEV-boost
  • Consensus Algorithms for Blockchains: peer-reviewed survey of PoW, PoS, and DPoS with a from-scratch implementation, published in Olympiads in Informatics

Open Source

  • zkUniswap: UniswapV3 fork with RISC Zero zk co-processor and on-chain Groth16 proof verification
  • sequencer-commitments: OP Stack hack introducing programmable commitments for L2 sequencers

  • transience: reentrancy-aware transient storage for Solidity (ethereum-optimism)
  • sandwich-mitigating-uniswap-v2: O(1) enforcement of the Greedy Sequencing Rule, making sandwich attacks unprofitable
  • multistaker: stake across protocols and chains from a single contract, with L1-L2 reward distribution
  • prime-field-arithmetic: secp256k1 field arithmetic in Solidity
  • quark: cash-settled NFT derivatives with on-chain pricing, no oracles
  • plonkish-solidity: verify halo2 circuits inside the EVM
